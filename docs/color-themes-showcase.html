📐 Design Principles — Syntax Color Theory

How well-designed syntax themes assign color

The best syntax color maps (Solarized, Nord, Dracula, Base16) share three rules: (1) Hue spacing — accent colors are spaced ~50° apart on the hue wheel so they are perceptually distinct even at a glance. (2) Equal luminance weight — no single metric "screams" louder than another; lightness is calibrated in L*a*b* perceptual space so all tokens have similar visual weight. (3) Semantic mapping — warm hues (red, orange, amber) signal urgency or physical load; cool hues (blue, teal, purple) signal measurement and cycles. WOD Wiki's 7 metrics map naturally to the full hue wheel at roughly 50° intervals.
