# Yoga4Strength — Power Vinyasa Session 2

**Source:** DoYogaWithMe  
**Feed:** yoga  
**Type:** Vinyasa / Strength Hybrid  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Level:** Intermediate

## Sequence

**Centering + Pranayama (3 min)**
- Comfortable seat, eyes closed
- Ujjayi breath: constrict back of throat, ocean sound on inhale and exhale

**Sun Salutation A — *Surya Namaskar A* (5 min)**
- 4 rounds, breath-linked
- Emphasis: slow Chaturanga — lower with control, elbows track back

**Sun Salutation B (5 min)**
- 4 rounds with Warrior I integration

**Strength Sequence (20 min)**
- Chair Pose — *Utkatasana*: 1 min hold × 2 (rest 30 sec between)
- Warrior III — *Virabhadrasana III*: 30 sec each side × 2
- Standing Splits: 30 sec each side
- Plank variations:
  - High Plank: 45 sec
  - Forearm Plank: 45 sec
  - Side Plank each side: 30 sec

**Arm Balance (5 min)**
- Crow Pose — *Bakasana*: 3–5 attempts, hold 5 breaths when you land it
- Chaturanga push-up flow: 3 × 5 slow reps (3 sec down, pause, press up)

**Standing Flow (10 min)**
- Warrior I → II → III full sequence: 2 rounds each side

**Core (5 min)**
- Boat Pose — *Navasana*: 3 × 10 breaths (lower to hover between sets)

**Cool-Down (7 min)**
- Pigeon Pose: 2 min each side
- Shoulder Stand or Legs-Up-the-Wall: 3 min

**Savasana (5 min)**

## Notes

The Chaturanga mechanics matter more than any other single pose in a power flow. Elbows to 90° max, forward of hands, body in one plank line. "The push-up that built yoga" — respect it. The Crow Pose attempts are more valuable than a 10-second hold: finding the balance point trains proprioception that transfers to everything else.
