# Juggernaut Training — Squat Day, Wave 1 (10s Accumulation)

**Source:** Chad Wesley Smith / Juggernaut Training Systems  
**Feed:** weightlifting  
**Type:** Powerlifting — Wave Periodization  
**Duration:** 75–90 minutes

## Workout

**Warm-Up**
- Foam roll quads/IT band/glutes: 5 min
- Box squat with pause: 3 × 5 (empty bar)
- Goblet squat: 2 × 10

**Main Sets — Back Squat**

*Example Training Max: 300 lb*

| Set | Load | Reps | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Warmup 1 | 45 lb | 10 | Bar |
| Warmup 2 | 135 lb | 5 | |
| Warmup 3 | 180 lb | 3 | 60% |
| Working 1–4 | 180 lb | 10 each | Hold form |
| Working 5 | 180 lb | **AMRAP** | Target 15+ reps |

**Assistance**

| Exercise | Sets × Reps |
|---|---|
| Romanian Deadlift | 3 × 10 (moderate) |
| Leg Press | 3 × 15 |
| Ab Wheel Rollout | 3 × 10 |

**Cool-Down**
- Hip flexor stretch 2 min each side
- Pigeon pose or figure-4 stretch

## WOD Syntax

```wod
(Warmup)
  10 Back Squat 45lb
  5 Back Squat 135lb
  3 Back Squat 180lb

(4)
  10 Back Squat 180lb

? Back Squat 180lb

(3)
  10 Romanian Deadlift

(3)
  15 Leg Press

(3)
  10 Ab Wheel Rollout
```

## Notes

The AMRAP set is the heart of Juggernaut. If you hit 15+ reps, your Training Max goes up for the next cycle. If you hit 12–14, it stays. If below 12, something is wrong — assess sleep, food, and fatigue. The high-rep accumulation wave builds the aerobic base under the barbell strength that peaks in Week 11 (Realization wave).
