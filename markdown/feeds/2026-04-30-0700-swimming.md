# Total Immersion Technique Focus — 1800m Drill Session

**Source:** Total Immersion (Terry Laughlin method)  
**Feed:** swimming  
**Type:** Technique / Drill  
**Distance:** 1800m  
**Duration:** ~45 minutes

## Workout

**Warm-Up — 300m**
- 300m easy freestyle, focus on relaxed exhale underwater

**Drill Block 1 — 400m (Superman / Balance Drills)**
- 4 × 100m on 2:30
  - 25m Superman glide (face-down, arms extended, feel balance point)
  - 25m Side balance (ear on shoulder, one arm extended)
  - 50m easy freestyle applying balance feel

**Drill Block 2 — 400m (Catch & Pull)**
- 4 × 100m on 2:30
  - 25m Fingertip drag (drag fingertips back along surface on recovery)
  - 25m Catch-up drill (one arm always extended until trailing hand "catches up")
  - 50m freestyle applying early vertical forearm

**Integration Set — 500m**
- 5 × 100m on 2:00
  - Swim "TI-style": 2-beat kick, long spine, patient hand entry
  - Focus on Strokes Per Length (SPL) — aim to reduce SPL by 1–2 from warm-up

**Cool-Down — 200m**
- 200m backstroke, long slow strokes

## WOD Syntax

```wod
(Warmup)
  300m Swim

(4) 2:30
  + 25m Superman Glide
  + 25m Side Balance
  + 50m Swim

(4) 2:30
  + 25m Fingertip Drag
  + 25m Catchup Drill
  + 50m Swim

(5) 2:00
  100m Swim

(Cooldown)
  200m Backstroke
```

## Notes

Count your strokes per length each rep. A lower SPL with the same speed = better efficiency. TI target for a 25-yard pool: 14–17 SPL for adults. Don't rush the glide.
