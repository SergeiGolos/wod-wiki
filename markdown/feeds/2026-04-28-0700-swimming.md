# Endurance Builder — 2500m Beginner Swim

**Source:** SwimSwam / Olivier Poirier-Leroy  
**Feed:** swimming  
**Type:** Aerobic Endurance  
**Distance:** 2500m  
**Duration:** ~50–60 minutes

## Workout

**Warm-Up — 400m**
- 200m easy freestyle (focus on long stroke, no rushing)
- 4 × 50m kick on 1:15 (board or streamline)

**Pre-Main — 400m**
- 4 × 100m on 2:00
  - Odds: freestyle, concentrate on bilateral breathing
  - Evens: backstroke, recover the shoulders

**Main Set — 1200m**
- 3 × 400m freestyle on 7:30
  - Descend effort across the three: aerobic → aerobic-threshold → threshold
  - Rest: remainder of 7:30 interval

**Cool-Down — 500m**
- 200m easy choice stroke
- 4 × 50m on 1:30 (drill: fingertip drag or catch-up)
- 100m easy backstroke

## WOD Syntax

```wod
(Warmup)
  200m Swim
  (4)
    50m Kick
    :15 Rest

(4) 2:00
  100m Swim

(3) 7:30
  400m Swim

(Cooldown)
  200m Swim
  (4)
    50m Drill
    :30 Rest
  100m Backstroke
```

## Notes

The descending-effort 400s are the key set — each one should feel slightly harder than the last. Use a tempo trainer or pace clock to stay honest. If 7:30 feels too tight, open to 8:00 and build down over weeks.
