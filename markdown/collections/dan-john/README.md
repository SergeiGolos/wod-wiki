# Dan John Kettlebell Workouts

**Category**: Strength & Conditioning  
**Founder**: Dan John  
**Philosophy**: "The goal is to keep the goal the goal." - Simple, effective programming with clear objectives.

---

## Overview

Dan John is a legendary strength coach, author, and former discus thrower whose influence on the fitness industry spans over four decades. His approach to training is characterized by elegant simplicity - focusing on fundamental movements, clear progressions, and sustainable programming that produces real-world results. Dan John's kettlebell programs have become legendary in the fitness community for their ability to build impressive strength, muscle, and conditioning with minimal equipment and time investment.

Dan John's philosophy centers on several key principles: the importance of the "armor" (building a resilient body), the value of pattern over perfection, the power of simplicity in programming, and the concept that "the goal is to keep the goal the goal." His workouts often use complexes and chains - sequences of movements performed without setting the weight down - to maximize training efficiency. He emphasizes that the best program is one you'll actually do consistently, leading to his preference for straightforward, repeatable protocols.

## Workout 1: 10,000 Swing Challenge

**Category**: Dan John Signature Program  
**Type**: Strength Endurance Challenge  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

The 10,000 Swing Challenge is Dan John's most famous kettlebell program, designed to build incredible work capacity, grip strength, and mental fortitude over four weeks. The program requires completing 500 swings per workout, four workouts per week, for a total of 10,000 swings. Between sets of swings, practitioners perform low-volume strength movements to maintain and build strength.

The program follows a structured approach with specific swing rep schemes per set, interspersed with strength movements like goblet squats, presses, or pull-ups. The high volume of swings develops powerful hips, a strong posterior chain, and exceptional conditioning. Many practitioners report significant improvements in body composition, work capacity, and mental toughness after completing the challenge.

```wod
(5)
  10 KB Swing 24kg
  15 KB Swing 24kg
  25 KB Swing 24kg
  50 KB Swing 24kg
  :30 Rest
  1 Goblet Squat 24kg

(5)
  10 KB Swing 24kg
  15 KB Swing 24kg
  25 KB Swing 24kg
  50 KB Swing 24kg
  :30 Rest
  2 Goblet Squat 24kg

(5)
  10 KB Swing 24kg
  15 KB Swing 24kg
  25 KB Swing 24kg
  50 KB Swing 24kg
  :30 Rest
  3 Goblet Squat 24kg

(5)
  10 KB Swing 24kg
  15 KB Swing 24kg
  25 KB Swing 24kg
  50 KB Swing 24kg
  :30 Rest
  4 Goblet Squat 24kg
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 20 workouts over 4 weeks
- **Exercises**: Two-Hand Kettlebell Swing, Goblet Squat (or press, pull-up)
- **Goal**: Complete 10,000 total swings

### Weekly Structure

| Week | Workouts | Total Swings |
|------|----------|--------------|
| Week 1 | 5 | 2,500 |
| Week 2 | 5 | 2,500 |
| Week 3 | 5 | 2,500 |
| Week 4 | 5 | 2,500 |

### Strength Movement Options (Between Swing Sets)

- Goblet Squats
- Overhead Press
- Pull-ups / Chin-ups
- Dips

### Collectible Metrics

- Total time per workout
- Weight used
- Strength movement progress

## Workout 2: Armor Building Complex (ABC)

**Category**: Dan John Complex  
**Type**: Strength Complex  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

The Armor Building Complex is Dan John's signature double kettlebell complex, originally designed as a "graduation workout" for the RKC kettlebell certification. The complex consists of 2 cleans, 1 press, and 3 front squats, performed as a continuous sequence without setting the bells down. This deceptively simple combination builds total-body strength, power, and conditioning.

The term "armor building" reflects the program's ability to develop a resilient, muscular physique that can withstand impact - valuable for contact sports and life in general. The complex targets the entire body: cleans develop explosive hip power, presses build overhead strength and shoulder stability, and front squats develop leg strength and core stability. When performed for multiple rounds with appropriate weight, the ABC creates significant metabolic demand.

```wod
(5)
  + 2 Double Clean 24kg
  + 1 Double Press 24kg
  + 3 Double Front Squat 24kg
  1:00 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 5 rounds
- **Exercises**: Double Clean, Double Press, Double Front Squat
- **Goal**: Complete all rounds with good form

### Standards

| Level | Weight (Men) | Weight (Women) | Rounds |
|-------|--------------|----------------|--------|
| Beginner | 2x16kg | 2x8kg | 3-5 |
| Intermediate | 2x24kg | 2x12kg | 5 |
| Advanced | 2x32kg | 2x16kg | 5+ |

### Collectible Metrics

- Rounds completed
- Weight used
- Time to complete

## Workout 3: Single Kettlebell Armor Building Complex

**Category**: Dan John Complex  
**Type**: Strength Complex  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate

### Description

This single-kettlebell version of the Armor Building Complex provides similar benefits to the double version while requiring only one bell. The complex alternates between left and right sides, building unilateral strength and addressing imbalances. This version is more accessible for those without matching pairs of kettlebells.

The single-kettlebell complex involves a clean and press on one side, a swing to switch hands, then a clean and press on the other side, followed by squats or additional movements. While the total load is reduced compared to doubles, the unilateral nature challenges stability and core strength in different ways.

```wod
(5)
  + 1 Clean & Press Left 24kg
  + 1 Swing
  + 1 Clean & Press Right 24kg
  + 2 Goblet Squat 24kg
  :45 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 5 rounds
- **Exercises**: Clean & Press, Swing, Goblet Squat
- **Goal**: Complete all rounds with good form

### Collectible Metrics

- Rounds completed
- Weight used
- Quality of movement

## Workout 4: The 40-Day Program

**Category**: Dan John Strength Program  
**Type**: Progressive Strength  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

The 40-Day Program is Dan John's approach to "Easy Strength" - building strength without excessive fatigue or soreness. The program uses sub-maximal weights performed frequently, typically 5 sets of 2 reps or 2 sets of 5 reps, done 5 days per week for 40 days. The focus is on perfect repetitions and building strength through frequency rather than intensity.

The beauty of the 40-Day Program lies in its sustainability. Because the weights are sub-maximal and the volume is moderate, recovery is rapid, allowing for daily practice. Over time, the frequent exposure to the movements builds neural efficiency, technical mastery, and surprisingly strong strength gains without the burnout associated with high-intensity programs.

```wod
(5) Easy Strength Day
  + 2 Double Clean 24kg
  + 2 Double Press 24kg
  + 2 Double Front Squat 24kg
  :60 Rest

(5) Pull-ups

(5) Hanging Leg Raises
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 40 consecutive days
- **Exercises**: Clean, Press, Front Squat, Pull-ups, Core
- **Goal**: Build strength without soreness

### Principles

1. Never miss a rep
2. Never strain excessively
3. Keep the same weight until 40 days complete
4. Practice, don't "work out"

### Collectible Metrics

- Daily consistency
- Quality of each rep
- Subjective energy levels

## Workout 5: The Mass Program

**Category**: Dan John Hypertrophy  
**Type**: High Volume Strength  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

The Mass Program is Dan John's approach to building muscle with kettlebells, combining the Armor Building Complex with strategic volume progression. The program uses a wave loading pattern, increasing volume over several weeks before deloading and repeating with heavier weights.

The program emphasizes compound movements and high tension, creating the metabolic and mechanical stress necessary for hypertrophy while maintaining the functional strength and movement quality that defines Dan John's approach. The combination of pressing, squatting, and pulling movements develops balanced musculature.

```wod
Week 1-2
(5)
  + 2 Double Clean 20kg
  + 1 Double Press 20kg
  + 3 Double Front Squat 20kg
  :60 Rest

Week 3-4
(5)
  + 2 Double Clean 24kg
  + 1 Double Press 24kg
  + 3 Double Front Squat 24kg
  :60 Rest

Week 5-6
(5)
  + 2 Double Clean 24kg
  + 2 Double Press 24kg
  + 3 Double Front Squat 24kg
  :60 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 6-12 weeks
- **Exercises**: Double Clean, Double Press, Double Front Squat
- **Goal**: Build muscle mass and strength

### Collectible Metrics

- Body weight
- Measurements
- Strength progression

## Workout 6: The Park Bench Program

**Category**: Dan John General Fitness  
**Type**: Maintenance / Easy Strength  
**Difficulty**: Beginner to Intermediate

### Description

The Park Bench Program represents Dan John's philosophy of "park bench" training - consistent, sustainable work that builds over time, as opposed to "bus bench" training where you're constantly waiting for results. The program is designed for long-term practice with minimal fatigue, perfect for those with busy lives who want to maintain strength and fitness.

The workout uses basic movement patterns with moderate volume, focusing on consistency over intensity. It can be performed 3-5 times per week indefinitely, making it ideal for maintenance phases or as a foundation for more intense training blocks.

```wod
(3) Park Bench
  5 Goblet Squat 24kg
  5 KB Swing 24kg
  5 Pushups
  :60 Rest

(3)
  3 Clean & Press Left 24kg
  3 Clean & Press Right 24kg
  :60 Rest

(3)
  5 KB Swing 24kg
  :30 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 15-20 minutes
- **Exercises**: Goblet Squat, Swing, Push-up, Clean & Press
- **Goal**: Consistent daily practice

### Collectible Metrics

- Weekly consistency
- Weight progression
- Energy levels
