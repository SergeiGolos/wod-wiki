# Girevoy Sport (Kettlebell Sport) Workouts

**Category**: Competitive Kettlebell Lifting  
**Origin**: Russia / Former Soviet Union  
**Philosophy**: "Maximum reps in minimum time." - Efficiency, relaxation, and endurance through competition lifts.

---

## Overview

Girevoy Sport (GS) is the competitive discipline of kettlebell lifting, originating in Russia and popular throughout the former Soviet Union. Unlike hardstyle kettlebell training which emphasizes maximum tension and power per rep, GS focuses on efficiency and relaxation to maximize repetitions within a fixed time frame. Competition lifts are performed for 10 minutes without setting the kettlebell down, testing both physical and mental endurance.

The three main competition events are the Jerk (two kettlebells pressed overhead from the rack position), the Snatch (one kettlebell swung from between the legs to overhead in one motion), and Long Cycle (clean and jerk with two kettlebells). Athletes compete in weight categories using competition-style kettlebells of fixed weights (8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28, 32 kg). The goal is to achieve the highest number of repetitions in 10 minutes without putting the kettlebells down.

## Workout 1: Biathlon (Jerk + Snatch)

**Category**: Girevoy Sport Competition  
**Type**: Competition Event  
**Difficulty**: Advanced

### Description

Biathlon is a two-event competition consisting of the Jerk followed by the Snatch. In competition, athletes perform a 10-minute jerk set with two kettlebells, rest for at least 30 minutes, then perform a 10-minute snatch set with one kettlebell. The total reps from both events determine the winner.

This workout simulates competition conditions, though training sets may be shorter than 10 minutes as athletes build capacity. The focus is on developing efficiency, pace, and the specific endurance required for each lift.

```wod
Biathlon Training
10:00 ? Double KB Jerk 24kg

30:00 Rest

10:00 ? KB Snatch 24kg
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 10 minutes per event
- **Exercises**: Double Kettlebell Jerk, Kettlebell Snatch
- **Goal**: Maximum reps in each 10-minute set

### Competition Standards

| Weight Class | Jerk Weight | Snatch Weight | Time |
|--------------|-------------|---------------|------|
| Men Amateur | 2x16kg | 16kg | 10:00 |
| Men Professional | 2x24kg | 24kg | 10:00 |
| Men Elite | 2x32kg | 32kg | 10:00 |
| Women Amateur | 16kg | 12kg | 10:00 |
| Women Professional | 20kg | 16kg | 10:00 |

### Collectible Metrics

- Jerk reps per set
- Snatch reps per set
- Pace (reps per minute)

## Workout 2: Long Cycle (Clean & Jerk)

**Category**: Girevoy Sport Competition  
**Type**: Competition Event  
**Difficulty**: Advanced

### Description

Long Cycle is a competition event consisting of the clean and jerk performed continuously for 10 minutes with two kettlebells. Each rep begins with the bells between the legs and ends with them overhead. Long Cycle is considered the most physically demanding of the GS events due to the additional clean in each repetition.

The event requires tremendous leg power for the clean, shoulder stability for the rack position, and overhead endurance. Efficient technique minimizes energy expenditure by using leg drive and relaxation in the rack position.

```wod
Long Cycle Training
10:00 ? Double KB Clean & Jerk 24kg
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 10 minutes continuous
- **Exercises**: Double Kettlebell Clean & Jerk
- **Goal**: Maximum reps in 10 minutes

### Competition Standards

| Level | Weight (Men) | Weight (Women) | Elite Standard |
|-------|--------------|----------------|----------------|
| Amateur | 2x16kg | 16kg | 60+ reps |
| Professional | 2x24kg | 20kg | 50+ reps |
| Elite | 2x32kg | 24kg | 40+ reps |

### Collectible Metrics

- Total reps in 10 minutes
- Pace maintenance
- Rest positions used

## Workout 3: Snatch Endurance

**Category**: Girevoy Sport Training  
**Type**: Snatch-Specific Endurance  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

Snatch endurance training develops the capacity to maintain the snatch for extended durations. Training approaches vary from shorter, intense sets to longer, sub-maximal efforts. The goal is to build both the muscular endurance and technical efficiency to maintain pace for 10 minutes.

Training sets may use various structures: fixed time sets, rep-based sets, or pace-based training. Key technical elements include relaxation in the overhead position, efficient drop technique, and hand transitions that minimize grip fatigue.

```wod
Snatch Endurance Session
(5)
  1:00 KB Snatch Left 16kg
  :30 Rest
  1:00 KB Snatch Right 16kg
  :30 Rest

Build Phase
(3)
  3:00 KB Snatch 16kg
  1:00 Rest

Test
5:00 ? KB Snatch 16kg
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 25-30 minutes
- **Exercises**: Kettlebell Snatch
- **Goal**: Build snatch endurance progressively

### Pace Targets

| Level | Pace (rpm) | 10-min Target |
|-------|------------|---------------|
| Beginner | 8-10 rpm | 80-100 reps |
| Intermediate | 10-12 rpm | 100-120 reps |
| Advanced | 12-14 rpm | 120-140 reps |
| Elite | 14+ rpm | 140+ reps |

### Collectible Metrics

- Minutes continuous
- Pace per minute
- Hand transition efficiency

## Workout 4: Jerk Technique and Endurance

**Category**: Girevoy Sport Training  
**Type**: Jerk-Specific Development  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

Jerk training develops the specific endurance to maintain two kettlebells overhead for 10 minutes. The jerk uses leg drive to propel the bells overhead, with a second dip to receive them. Efficiency comes from relaxation in the rack position and minimal energy expenditure in the overhead fixation.

Training progresses from short sets focusing on technique to longer sets building competition endurance. The rack position - holding the bells against the chest - is crucial for recovery between reps and must be developed alongside the overhead work.

```wod
Jerk Technique Session
(10)
  1 Double KB Jerk 24kg
  :30 Rest

Jerk Endurance
(5)
  2:00 Double KB Jerk 20kg
  1:00 Rest

Competition Prep
(3)
  5:00 Double KB Jerk 24kg
  3:00 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 30-40 minutes
- **Exercises**: Double Kettlebell Jerk
- **Goal**: Build jerk capacity

### Technical Elements

- Second dip timing
- Rack position relaxation
- Overhead fixation
- Breathing pattern

### Collectible Metrics

- Reps per set
- Duration achieved
- Technical efficiency

## Workout 5: 4-Week Beginner Girevoy Sport Program

**Category**: Girevoy Sport Beginner  
**Type**: Progressive Introduction  
**Difficulty**: Beginner

### Description

This 4-week program introduces beginners to Girevoy Sport training. The program uses lighter weights and shorter sets to build technique and endurance progressively. Each week increases either volume or duration while maintaining focus on technical efficiency.

The program trains three days per week with emphasis on the competition lifts plus supporting exercises. By the end of 4 weeks, practitioners should be able to maintain each lift for at least 5 minutes with basic efficiency.

```wod
Week 1
Day 1
  (3) 1:00 Double KB Jerk 16kg, :60 Rest
  (3) 1:00 KB Snatch Left 12kg, :30 Rest, 1:00 KB Snatch Right 12kg, :30 Rest
Day 2
  (5) 10 KB Swing 16kg, :30 Rest
  (3) 1:00 Double KB Clean 16kg, :60 Rest
Day 3
  (3) 2:00 Double KB Jerk 16kg, 2:00 Rest
  (3) 1:30 KB Snatch 12kg per arm, 1:00 Rest

Week 2
Day 1
  (3) 1:30 Double KB Jerk 16kg, :60 Rest
  (3) 1:30 KB Snatch 12kg per arm, :45 Rest
Day 2
  (5) 15 KB Swing 16kg, :30 Rest
  (3) 1:30 Double KB Clean 16kg, :60 Rest
Day 3
  (3) 3:00 Double KB Jerk 16kg, 3:00 Rest
  (3) 2:00 KB Snatch 12kg per arm, 1:00 Rest

Week 3
Day 1
  (3) 2:00 Double KB Jerk 16kg, :90 Rest
  (3) 2:00 KB Snatch 12kg per arm, :60 Rest
Day 2
  (5) 20 KB Swing 16kg, :30 Rest
  (3) 2:00 Double KB Clean & Jerk 16kg, :90 Rest
Day 3
  (2) 4:00 Double KB Jerk 16kg, 4:00 Rest
  (2) 3:00 KB Snatch 12kg per arm, 2:00 Rest

Week 4
Day 1
  (2) 3:00 Double KB Jerk 16kg, 2:00 Rest
  (2) 3:00 KB Snatch 12kg per arm, 1:00 Rest
Day 2
  (5) 25 KB Swing 16kg, :30 Rest
  (3) 2:00 Double KB Clean & Jerk 16kg, :90 Rest
Day 3
  5:00 Double KB Jerk 16kg
  5:00 Rest
  5:00 KB Snatch 12kg
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 4 weeks, 3 days per week
- **Exercises**: Jerk, Snatch, Clean, Swing
- **Goal**: Build foundation for GS competition

### Collectible Metrics

- Set duration progression
- Weight progression
- Technical efficiency

## Workout 6: Long Cycle Training Session

**Category**: Girevoy Sport Training  
**Type**: Long Cycle Development  
**Difficulty**: Advanced

### Description

Long Cycle training sessions develop the specific endurance for the clean and jerk event. The clean adds significant leg and back fatigue compared to pure jerk training, requiring dedicated conditioning. Training alternates between technique-focused work and endurance building.

Sessions may use interval structures, pace training, or continuous sets depending on the training phase. Key technical elements include efficient rack recovery, leg drive utilization, and grip conservation through proper hand insertion.

```wod
Long Cycle Session
Technique
(10)
  1 Double KB Clean & Jerk 24kg
  :30 Rest

Intervals
(5)
  1:00 Double KB Clean & Jerk 20kg
  :30 Rest

Tempo Work
(3)
  3:00 Double KB Clean & Jerk 24kg
  2:00 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 30-35 minutes
- **Exercises**: Double Kettlebell Clean & Jerk
- **Goal**: Build Long Cycle capacity

### Pace Strategy

| Phase | Pace | Purpose |
|-------|------|---------|
| Minutes 1-3 | 6-8 rpm | Establish rhythm |
| Minutes 4-7 | 5-6 rpm | Maintain efficiency |
| Minutes 8-10 | 4-6 rpm | Survive to finish |

### Collectible Metrics

- Reps per interval
- Total duration achieved
- Pace degradation

## Workout 7: Relay Competition Format

**Category**: Girevoy Sport Team Competition  
**Type**: Team Event  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

Relay competitions in Girevoy Sport involve teams of athletes performing short, intense sets in succession. Each team member typically lifts for 3 minutes before passing to the next athlete. The team with the highest total reps wins.

Relay training focuses on high-intensity short sets with quick transitions. This format is more accessible than traditional 10-minute sets and is often used in local competitions to introduce new athletes to GS.

```wod
Relay Training
Team Format
(4) Athletes rotate
  3:00 Double KB Jerk 24kg
  :30 Transition

Individual Practice
(5)
  3:00 Double KB Jerk 24kg
  2:00 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 3 minutes per athlete
- **Exercises**: Competition lifts (Jerk or Long Cycle typically)
- **Goal**: Maximum team reps

### Team Strategy

- Order athletes by pace consistency
- Fastest athletes in anchor positions
- Transitions practiced for speed

### Collectible Metrics

- Individual reps per 3-minute set
- Transition time
- Team total

## Competition Rules Summary

### Time Limits
- All events: 10 minutes maximum
- No setting the kettlebell down allowed
- Count continues while bell is at rest position

### Valid Reps
- **Jerk**: Both bells fixed overhead with straight arms and legs
- **Snatch**: Bell fixed overhead with straight arm, visible fixation
- **Long Cycle**: Clean + Jerk counted as one rep

### Weight Categories
Competition kettlebells come in standard weights regardless of size (competition bells are all the same dimensions):
- 8, 12, 16, 20, 24, 28, 32 kg

### Scoring
- Total reps in 10 minutes
- If multiple attempts, best score counts
- Biathlon: Jerk + Snatch combined
