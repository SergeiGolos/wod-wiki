## Workout 3: Quick and the Dead

**Category**: StrongFirst Conditioning  
**Type**: AMRAP  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate

### Description

Quick and the Dead is a high-intensity conditioning protocol developed by Pavel Tsatsouline that combines explosive kettlebell swings with push-ups in a time-efficient format. The program is designed to develop alactic power - the ability to generate maximum force in short bursts - along with alactic capacity, the ability to repeat those bursts with minimal recovery.

The workout uses a specific work-to-rest ratio, typically 15 seconds of work followed by 15 seconds of rest, repeated for multiple rounds. This interval structure targets the alactic energy system while providing just enough recovery to maintain power output throughout the session. The minimalist design makes it an excellent stand-alone conditioning program or a complement to strength-focused training.

```wod
(10)
  :15 KB Swing 24kg
  :15 Rest

(10)
  :15 Pushups
  :15 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 20 minutes total
- **Exercises**: Two-Hand Kettlebell Swing, Push-ups
- **Goal**: Maintain maximum power output throughout

### Collectible Metrics

- Total reps per round
- Quality of movement throughout
