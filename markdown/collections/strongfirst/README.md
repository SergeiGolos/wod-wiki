# StrongFirst Kettlebell Workouts

**Category**: StrongFirst / Hardstyle Kettlebell  
**Founder**: Pavel Tsatsouline  
**Philosophy**: "Simple. Sinister. Effective." - Focus on tension, power, and precision over volume.

---

## Overview

StrongFirst is the premier kettlebell certification and training organization founded by Pavel Tsatsouline, widely recognized as the father of the modern kettlebell movement in the West. The StrongFirst methodology emphasizes the "hardstyle" approach to kettlebell training, which prioritizes high-tension techniques, powerful hip drives, and efficient energy transfer. Unlike sport-style kettlebell lifting (Girevoy Sport), hardstyle training focuses on generating maximum force with each repetition rather than maximizing reps over time. This approach develops exceptional strength, power, and conditioning while reinforcing proper movement patterns that carry over to athletics and daily life.

The StrongFirst philosophy is built around several key principles: intra-abdominal pressure for spinal stability, biomechanical breathing match (exhaling on effort), irradiation (radiating tension throughout the body), and the concept of "greasing the groove" - practicing skills frequently with fresh neural pathways rather than training to failure. Their programming is designed to be sustainable, leaving the practitioner with energy for their primary sport, profession, and daily responsibilities while still building impressive strength and conditioning.

## Workout 1: Simple & Sinister

**Category**: StrongFirst Benchmark  
**Type**: For Time (with time standards)  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

Simple & Sinister is Pavel Tsatsouline's flagship program, designed to develop foundational strength and conditioning with minimal complexity. The workout consists of 100 one-arm kettlebell swings followed by 10 Turkish get-ups (5 per side). This deceptively simple combination builds powerful hips, a resilient back, strong shoulders, and exceptional core stability. The program is designed to be practiced daily or near-daily, with the goal of progressively increasing the kettlebell weight and decreasing the time to completion.

The "Simple" standard represents a respectable level of strength and conditioning that most dedicated practitioners can achieve with consistent training. The "Sinister" standard represents elite performance, requiring exceptional power, conditioning, and mental fortitude. Both standards have specific weight and time requirements that serve as clear, measurable goals for practitioners.

```wod
5:00 AMRAP
  10 KB Swing 32kg
  :30 Rest

1:00 Rest

(10) :60
  1 Turkish Get Up 24kg
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 5 minutes swings + 10 minutes get-ups
- **Exercises**: One-Arm Kettlebell Swing, Turkish Get-Up
- **Goal**: Complete 100 swings in 5 minutes, then 10 get-ups in 10 minutes

### Standards

| Level | Swings Weight | Get-Up Weight | Time Standard |
|-------|---------------|---------------|---------------|
| Simple (Men) | 32kg | 24kg | 5 min swings, 10 min TGU |
| Simple (Women) | 16kg | 12kg | 5 min swings, 10 min TGU |
| Sinister (Men) | 48kg | 32kg | 5 min swings, 10 min TGU |
| Sinister (Women) | 24kg | 16kg | 5 min swings, 10 min TGU |

### Collectible Metrics

- Time to complete 100 swings
- Time to complete 10 Turkish Get-Ups
- Weight achieved for each movement

## Workout 2: The Rite of Passage

**Category**: StrongFirst Strength Program  
**Type**: Progressive Strength  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

The Rite of Passage is StrongFirst's premier press-focused program, designed to build impressive overhead pressing strength using a structured laddering approach. The program uses "ladders" - progressively increasing rep schemes that allow for high volume without excessive fatigue. A typical ladder progresses from 1 rep to 2 reps to 3 reps, then resets. This structure allows for significant total volume while maintaining quality throughout each set.

The program progresses through light, medium, and heavy days, incorporating clean and press ladders along with pull-up or chin-up work. Over weeks and months, practitioners progressively increase the height of their ladders and the weight of their kettlebell, building substantial pressing power. The Rite of Passage has produced countless practitioners capable of pressing half their bodyweight or more overhead with a single kettlebell.

```wod
(5) Light Day
  + 1 Clean & Press
  + 2 Clean & Press
  + 3 Clean & Press
  :60 Rest

(3) Pullups

(5) Medium Day
  + 1 Clean & Press
  + 2 Clean & Press
  + 3 Clean & Press
  :60 Rest

(3) Pullups

(3) Heavy Day
  + 1 Clean & Press
  + 2 Clean & Press
  + 3 Clean & Press
  + 4 Clean & Press
  + 5 Clean & Press
  :60 Rest

(5) Pullups
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: Variable based on ladder height
- **Exercises**: Clean & Press, Pull-ups
- **Goal**: Build to pressing a kettlebell half your bodyweight

### Collectible Metrics

- Maximum ladder height achieved
- Weight pressed
- Total volume per session

## Workout 3: Quick and the Dead

**Category**: StrongFirst Conditioning  
**Type**: AMRAP  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate

### Description

Quick and the Dead is a high-intensity conditioning protocol developed by Pavel Tsatsouline that combines explosive kettlebell swings with push-ups in a time-efficient format. The program is designed to develop alactic power - the ability to generate maximum force in short bursts - along with alactic capacity, the ability to repeat those bursts with minimal recovery.

The workout uses a specific work-to-rest ratio, typically 15 seconds of work followed by 15 seconds of rest, repeated for multiple rounds. This interval structure targets the alactic energy system while providing just enough recovery to maintain power output throughout the session. The minimalist design makes it an excellent stand-alone conditioning program or a complement to strength-focused training.

```wod
(10)
  :15 KB Swing 24kg
  :15 Rest

(10)
  :15 Pushups
  :15 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 20 minutes total
- **Exercises**: Two-Hand Kettlebell Swing, Push-ups
- **Goal**: Maintain maximum power output throughout

### Collectible Metrics

- Total reps per round
- Quality of movement throughout

## Workout 4: The Eagle

**Category**: StrongFirst Strength Endurance  
**Type**: For Time  
**Difficulty**: Advanced

### Description

The Eagle is a challenging benchmark workout that tests both strength endurance and mental fortitude. This workout combines pressing strength with pulling power, creating a balanced upper-body challenge that reveals weaknesses in pressing endurance, pulling capacity, or grip strength.

The workout consists of a series of clean and press complexes paired with pull-ups, performed for time. The volume is significant, requiring practitioners to maintain technique and efficiency throughout. Success in The Eagle requires not only raw strength but also the ability to pace oneself and maintain composure under fatigue.

```wod
(5)
  5 Clean & Press 24kg
  5 Pullups

(5)
  4 Clean & Press 24kg
  4 Pullups

(5)
  3 Clean & Press 24kg
  3 Pullups

(5)
  2 Clean & Press 24kg
  2 Pullups

(5)
  1 Clean & Press 24kg
  1 Pullup
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 5 rounds at each rep scheme
- **Exercises**: Clean & Press, Pull-ups
- **Goal**: Complete all reps as fast as possible with good form

### Collectible Metrics

- Total time to completion
- Quality maintained throughout

## Workout 5: StrongFirst Snatch Test

**Category**: StrongFirst Certification Standard  
**Type**: Max Reps  
**Difficulty**: Advanced

### Description

The StrongFirst Snatch Test is the certification standard for StrongFirst SFG (StrongFirst Girya) instructors. This test requires 100 snatches in 5 minutes with a 24kg kettlebell for men or 12kg for women. The test is typically performed as part of the certification weekend, where candidates must demonstrate not only the physical capacity to complete the test but also technical proficiency and safety throughout.

The snatch test challenges both the alactic and aerobic energy systems, requiring explosive power in each snatch while developing significant cardiovascular demand over the five minutes. Pacing strategies vary, but most successful candidates use a structured approach with planned rest periods, often breaking the 100 reps into sets with brief pauses in the rack or overhead position.

```wod
5:00 ? KB Snatch 24kg
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 5 minutes continuous
- **Exercises**: Kettlebell Snatch
- **Goal**: Complete 100 reps in 5 minutes

### Standards

| Gender | Weight | Reps | Time |
|--------|--------|------|------|
| Men | 24kg | 100 | 5:00 |
| Women | 12kg | 100 | 5:00 |

### Collectible Metrics

- Total reps in 5 minutes
- Time to complete 100 reps
