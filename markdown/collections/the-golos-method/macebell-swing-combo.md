# Macebell Swing Combos

**Category**: The Golos Method  
**Type**: Rotational Strength / Flow Work  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

## Description

The macebell swing combo is the rotational and shoulder-resilience pillar of the Golos Method. Using a steel mace — an unevenly loaded implement with a long lever arm — you perform a series of connected movements that challenge the body in planes traditional barbell and kettlebell work rarely touch.

The core combo consists of four foundational mace movements:

- **360 Swing**: The mace travels in a full circle around the head, developing shoulder mobility and grip endurance.
- **10-to-2 Swing**: A pendulum-like swing from the 10 o'clock to 2 o'clock positions, building anti-rotation core strength and lateral shoulder stability.
- **Gravedigger**: A vertical chopping motion from shoulder to hip, training powerful downward force and eccentric control.
- **Touchdown**: A dynamic squat-to-stand movement with the mace held overhead, linking lower-body power with overhead stability.

These movements are performed as a continuous flow or in structured sets, creating a conditioning stimulus that is equal parts strength, mobility, and coordination. The macebell does not allow cheating — the torque demands full-body tension on every rep.

## How to Train It

### Equipment Selection

- **Mace weight**: Start lighter than your ego suggests. Most experienced athletes begin with 10-15 lb. The long handle creates leverage that multiplies the effective load significantly.
- **Common progression**: 7 lb → 10 lb → 15 lb → 20 lb → 25 lb. Advance slowly — months at each weight are normal.
- **Grip**: Hold near the end of the handle to maximize the lever effect. As you fatigue, choke up slightly to reduce torque.

### Execution Cues

**360 Swing**
1. Start with the mace vertical, head pointing up, dominant hand near the end of the handle.
2. Swing the mace to one side, allowing momentum to carry it behind the head.
3. Guide the circular path with a packed shoulder and braced core.
4. Catch and redirect smoothly — there should be no jarring stop at any point in the circle.

**10-to-2 Swing**
1. Begin with the mace held horizontally across the body.
2. Pivot at the hips and allow the mace to swing laterally from 10 o'clock to 2 o'clock.
3. Resist rotation through the torso — the obliques and anti-rotators do the real work.
4. Keep the mace head at roughly eye level throughout the arc.

**Gravedigger**
1. Start with the mace held at shoulder height on one side.
2. Drive the mace downward in a chopping motion toward the opposite hip.
3. Control the descent — the eccentric load on the shoulder and lat is significant.
4. Return to the start with an upward pulling motion, keeping the elbow close to the body.

**Touchdown**
1. Hold the mace overhead with arms extended.
2. Squat deeply while keeping the mace stable above your head.
3. Touch the mace head to the ground (or as low as your mobility allows).
4. Stand explosively, returning to the overhead position.

### Programming Recommendations

**Beginner (Weeks 1-4)**
- Practice individual movements before linking them into flows
- 3-4 sets of 5 reps per side, 3 days per week
- Rest 60-90 seconds between sets

**Intermediate (Months 2-6)**
- Combine 360s and 10-to-2s into alternating flows
- Add gravediggers and touchdowns for variety
- Introduce EMOM protocols for conditioning

**Advanced (6+ months)**
- Perform extended flows (5-10 minutes continuous)
- Work with heavier maces (20+ lb)
- Integrate with kettlebell and sandbag work in combined sessions

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Starting with too heavy a mace (the most common and painful mistake)
- Allowing the shoulder to elevate or shrug during swings
- Using arm strength alone instead of whole-body rotation and core bracing
- Bending at the waist rather than rotating through the torso
- Training in cramped spaces with low ceilings or fragile objects nearby

## Safety Considerations

The macebell places unique stress on the shoulders, wrists, and lower back. Always warm up with light 360s before attempting heavier work or complex flows. ## Collectible Metrics

- **Reps per movement**: Track each element of the combo separately
- **Flow duration**: Minutes of continuous movement without setting the mace down
- **Weight used**: Mace weight for each session
- **Total combo rounds**: How many complete cycles of the four movements you perform
- **Quality rating**: Self-assess control and fluidity on a 1-10 scale
