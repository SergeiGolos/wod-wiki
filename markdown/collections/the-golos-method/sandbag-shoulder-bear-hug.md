# Sandbag Over-the-Shoulder & Bear-Hug Carries

**Category**: The Golos Method  
**Type**: Odd-Object Strength / Loaded Carries  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

## Description

Sandbag work is the odd-object and real-world strength pillar of the Golos Method. Unlike barbells and dumbbells, a sandbag is unstable, shifting, and awkward — which is precisely why it builds strength that transfers to grappling, manual labor, and outdoor work. The two primary movements in this pillar are the over-the-shoulder throw and the bear-hug carry.

The **over-the-shoulder** movement involves lifting a heavy sandbag from the ground, hoisting it onto one shoulder, and tossing it over that shoulder to the ground behind you. It is a full-body power movement that trains the posterior chain, grip, core bracing, and rotational force production. The asymmetry of the load forces the body to stabilize in ways a symmetrical barbell cannot replicate.

The **bear-hug carry** involves scooping the sandbag into your arms, clamping it against your chest in a tight embrace, and walking for distance or time. This isometric-dominant movement crushes the grip, taxes the biceps and forearms, demands constant core engagement to prevent the lower back from extending, and builds the mental toughness to hold on when every muscle is screaming to drop the load.

Together, these movements develop what strongman competitors call "farm strength" — the raw, unglamorous ability to pick up heavy, awkward things and move them.

## How to Train It

### Equipment Selection

- **Sandbag weight**: Beginners should start with 40-60 lb. Intermediate practitioners typically work with 80-100 lb. Advanced athletes use 120-150 lb or heavier. The instability of sand makes a given weight feel significantly heavier than an equivalent dumbbell or barbell.
- **Bag type**: A durable canvas or Cordura sandbag with multiple filler bags inside. This allows weight adjustment and reduces the risk of the bag bursting.
- **Surface**: Grass, dirt, rubber flooring, or astroturf. Avoid concrete if possible — it will destroy the bag and your elbows on the over-the-shoulder impact.
- **Space**: You need enough room to throw the bag overhead and behind you without hitting anything, plus a clear path for carries.

### Execution Cues

**Over-the-Shoulder**
1. **Setup**: Straddle the sandbag with feet wider than shoulder width. Hinge at the hips and grip the bag underneath with both hands, fingers intertwined if possible.
2. **Lift**: Deadlift the bag off the ground by extending the hips and knees. Keep the back flat and chest up.
3. **Hoist to shoulder**: As the bag rises above the knees, explosively extend the hips and pull the bag upward, transitioning your grip to scoop the bag onto one shoulder.
4. **Toss**: Once the bag is stable on the shoulder, dip slightly and drive the bag up and back, throwing it over the shoulder. Use hip extension and a strong push from the shoulder to generate momentum.
5. **Reset**: Turn around, straddle the bag, and repeat. Alternate shoulders each rep to balance the rotational demand.

**Bear-Hug Carry**
1. **Pick up**: Deadlift the sandbag and scoop it into your arms, clamping it tight against your chest. Your arms should wrap around the bag like a bear hug — hence the name.
2. **Posture**: Stand tall with a neutral spine. Resist the urge to lean back — the bag will try to pull you into extension. Brace your core hard.
3. **Walk**: Take short, controlled steps. The bag will shift and settle with each step — adjust your grip and posture continuously to maintain control.
4. **Breathe**: Small, sharp breaths while maintaining abdominal tension. Do not fully relax the brace between breaths.
5. **Finish**: Set the bag down under control. Do not drop it from chest height.

### Programming Recommendations

**Beginner (learning the movements)**
- Practice the over-the-shoulder throw with a light bag: 3-4 sets of 3 reps per side
- Bear-hug carry: 2-3 sets of 20-30 meters
- Focus on body mechanics rather than load or speed

**Intermediate (building work capacity)**
- Superset 5 over-the-shoulder reps with a 40-meter carry, resting 2 minutes between rounds
- Perform 3-4 rounds, 2 days per week
- Increase bag weight by 10-15 lb when you can complete all rounds with control

**Advanced (strength and endurance)**
- AMRAP-style blocks: 10-15 minutes of alternating throws and carries
- Heavy carry tests: max distance with 120-150 lb without dropping
- Combine with farmer's carries or yoke work for a full loaded-carry session

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Using a bag that is too heavy before mastering the hip-hinge and toss mechanics
- Rounding the lower back during the initial lift
- Leaning backward during the bear-hug carry (indicates the bag is too heavy or core is too weak)
- Holding breath for too long during carries (increases blood pressure and causes early fatigue)
- Dropping the bag from shoulder or chest height (damages the bag and risks injury)
- Training on hard surfaces where the bag or your body will absorb impact poorly

## Safety Considerations

Sandbag training is deceptively demanding on the lower back, grip, and cardiovascular system. The combination of heavy lifting, rotational throwing, and sustained isometric carries places significant systemic load. If you feel your lower back rounding during the lift or toss, stop immediately and reduce weight. Grip failure on a heavy carry can result in the bag falling on your feet or knees — know when to set it down safely. Those with herniated discs or other spinal issues should consult a medical professional before performing over-the-shoulder throws. Always ensure adequate clearance behind you before throwing the bag.

## Collectible Metrics

- **Over-the-shoulder reps**: Total reps per session, broken down by side
- **Sandbag weight**: Load used for each movement
- **Carry distance**: Total meters walked in bear-hug position
- **Carry time**: How long you can hold the bag without setting it down
- **Total rounds**: For AMRAP or density-style sessions
- **Quality rating**: Self-assess lift mechanics, posture during carries, and control on drops
