# Grease the Groove — Pull-Ups

**Category**: The Golos Method  
**Type**: High-Frequency Strength / Skill Work  
**Difficulty**: Beginner to Advanced

## Description

Grease the Groove (GTG) is a high-frequency, sub-maximal training method popularized by Pavel Tsatsouline. The core principle: strength is a skill, and skills are best practiced often and fresh. Instead of training pull-ups to failure in a single session, you perform multiple easy sets spread across the entire day — never allowing fatigue to degrade your movement quality.

In the Golos Method, GTG pull-ups serve as the foundational pulling practice. A doorframe pull-up bar, a park bar, or any overhead anchor becomes your gym. Sets are performed at roughly 50-70% of your maximum fresh effort, leaving plenty of reps in reserve. Over weeks and months, this daily accumulation of quality reps builds tendon resilience, motor pattern efficiency, and raw pulling volume that translates directly to a higher one-rep max.

The beauty of GTG is its flexibility. It requires no warm-up, no equipment beyond a bar, and no dedicated block of time. It is the perfect complement to the more structured EMOM and implement work in the method.

### Beginner — Building the First Rep
```wod
(8) 1:00 EMOM
  + 1-2 Assisted Pullup or Negatives
```

### Intermediate — Daily GTG
```wod
(5)
  5 Pullups
  : as needed
```
*Perform one set every 60-90 minutes during waking hours. Do not go to failure.*

### Advanced — Weighted GTG
```wod
(6)
  3 Weighted Pullup 10-20lb
  : as needed
```
*Spread across the day. Stop every set with at least 2 reps in reserve.*

### Volume Accumulation Test
```wod
20:00 ?
  Pullups
```
*Perform sets of 2-3 every 2-3 minutes. Goal: accumulate 30-50 quality reps without form breakdown.*

## How to Train It

### Equipment Selection

- **Pull-up bar**: Sturdy doorframe bar, wall-mounted bar, or park equipment. Must not shift under load.
- **Weight belt or vest**: For advanced practitioners adding external load. Start with 5-10 lb and progress slowly.
- **Bands or bench**: For beginners who cannot yet perform a full pull-up. Use assistance to complete full-range reps.

### Setup and Execution

1. **Grip**: Slightly wider than shoulder width, palms facing away (standard) or toward you (chin-up variation). Dead hang at the bottom with active shoulders.
2. **Initiate**: Drive elbows down and back, pulling chest toward the bar. Think of bringing your shoulder blades into your back pockets.
3. **Top position**: Chin clearly over the bar. Brief pause to demonstrate control.
4. **Lower with control**: Resist gravity on the way down. The eccentric phase builds significant strength.
5. **Dead hang reset**: Allow arms to fully extend, then immediately initiate the next rep. No swinging or kipping.

### Programming Recommendations

**Beginner (0-5 strict reps)**
- Use band assistance or negatives (3-5 second lowers)
- GTG sets of 1-2 reps, 5-8 times per day
- Focus on full range of motion over rep count

**Intermediate (5-12 strict reps)**
- GTG sets of 3-5 reps, 5-6 times per day
- Add a dedicated test day once per week: max fresh set
- Introduce weight once you can perform 10 strict reps with ease

**Advanced (12+ strict reps)**
- GTG sets of 4-6 weighted reps, 4-5 times per day
- Alternate between weighted GTG days and high-volume bodyweight days
- Track weekly volume and aim for gradual progression (10-20% volume increase per month)

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Going to failure on GTG sets (defeats the purpose)
- Skipping the dead hang (reduces range of motion and lat activation)
- Kipping or swinging to squeeze out extra reps
- Training GTG on the same day as a separate high-volume back workout (conflicts with recovery)
- Neglecting scapular pull-ups and shoulder health work

## Safety Considerations

Shoulder impingement and elbow tendinopathy are the most common issues with high-frequency pull-up training. If you experience sharp shoulder pain during the pull, stop immediately and assess your grip width and shoulder position. Incorporate dead hangs, scapular pull-ups, and face pulls to maintain shoulder health. If elbow pain develops, reduce volume and ensure you are not bouncing out of the bottom position.

## Collectible Metrics

- **Daily volume**: Total pull-ups completed across all GTG sets
- **Max fresh set**: Single best set performed when fully rested (test weekly)
- **Weekly total**: Cumulative reps across seven days
- **Weight used**: External load on weighted GTG sets
- **Quality rating**: Self-assess form on a 1-10 scale each set
