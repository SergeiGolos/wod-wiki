# The Golos Method

**Category**: Daily Strength & Conditioning  
**Type**: Unconventional / High-Frequency / Minimalist  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

---

## Overview

The Golos Method is a daily minimalist strength protocol built around high-frequency, sub-maximal training with unconventional implements. Rather than grinding through long sessions a few days per week, the method spreads focused work across the entire day — greasing the groove on pull-ups, hitting a concentrated EMOM block for pressing, and rotating through implement-specific conditioning with macebells, kettlebells, and sandbags.

The philosophy is simple: consistency beats intensity. Small doses done every day accumulate into extraordinary capacity. The method prioritizes movement quality, joint health, and work capacity over peak performance on any single session. It is designed for practitioners who want to stay strong, mobile, and resilient without living in the gym.

## The Five Pillars

### 1. Grease the Groove — Pull-Ups
Sub-maximal pull-up sets performed intermittently throughout the day. Never to failure. The goal is to ingrain the motor pattern and build volume without fatigue compromising form.

### 2. EMOM Weighted Push-Ups
Ten minutes, every minute on the minute, of weighted push-ups. A compressed block of pressing volume that builds chest, shoulder, and triceps endurance while training the body to recover under load.

### 3. Macebell Swing Combos
Rotational steel-mace work combining 360 swings, 10-to-2 swings, gravediggers, and touchdowns. Develops shoulder resilience, anti-rotation core strength, and grip endurance in planes that barbells cannot touch.

### 4. Kettlebell Clean & Press / Snatch
Ballistic kettlebell work for full-body power and conditioning. The clean & press builds raw strength and stability; the snatch delivers peak cardiovascular demand in a single movement.

### 5. Sandbag Over-the-Shoulder & Bear-Hug Carries
Odd-object lifting and loaded carries with an unstable sandbag. Builds real-world strength, core bracing, and grip integrity that transfers directly to grappling, manual labor, and outdoor work.

## How to Use This Collection

Each pillar has its own detailed file containing:

- Movement explanation and benefits
- Whiteboard workout scripts (beginner through advanced)
- Equipment selection guidance
- Technique cues and safety considerations
- Collectible metrics for tracking progress

Rotate through the implements daily or stack complementary pieces in a single session. There is no rigid week template — the method rewards showing up and doing the work, however the day allows.

## Collectible Metrics

- **Pull-ups**: Daily volume (reps), max fresh set, weekly total
- **Push-ups**: Reps per EMOM round, weight used, total reps per session
- **Macebell**: Reps per movement, weight, flow duration
- **Kettlebell**: Reps per set, weight, pace (rpm), total volume
- **Sandbag**: Loads used, carry distance/time, reps over shoulder
