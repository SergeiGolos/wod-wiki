# EMOM Weighted Push-Ups

**Category**: The Golos Method  
**Type**: EMOM / Strength-Endurance  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

## Description

The 10-minute EMOM of weighted push-ups is the pressing pillar of the Golos Method. Every minute on the minute, you drop and perform a set of push-ups with added load — typically a weight plate on your back, a weighted vest, or a sandbag. When the reps are complete, the remaining time in that minute is your rest. When the next minute begins, you go again.

This format creates a unique stimulus: sustained sub-maximal strength work under time pressure. Unlike traditional sets with long rest periods, the EMOM forces your body to recover while loaded and under the clock. The result is a dense block of pressing volume that builds chest, shoulder, and triceps endurance while reinforcing core bracing and horizontal pressing mechanics.

Ten minutes is long enough to accumulate significant volume, short enough to maintain intensity, and repeatable enough to perform daily without excessive fatigue. The goal is not to reach failure in any given minute — it is to hold a consistent rep count across all 10 rounds.

## How to Train It

### Equipment Selection

- **Weight plate**: A standard Olympic plate placed on your upper back. Requires a partner or careful self-placement.
- **Weighted vest**: The most convenient option. Allows hands-free loading and easy weight adjustment.
- **Sandbag or backpack**: An odd-object alternative. The instability adds a core challenge but makes loading less precise.
- **Floor surface**: A firm, non-slippery surface. A yoga mat or exercise mat provides grip and wrist comfort.

### Setup and Execution

1. **Hand placement**: Slightly wider than shoulder width, fingers spread, wrists stacked under elbows.
2. **Body line**: Maintain a rigid plank from head to heels. Do not let hips sag or pike upward.
3. **Lower with control**: Chest touches the floor or comes within a fist's height. Elbows track at roughly a 45-degree angle from the torso — not flared to 90 degrees.
4. **Press through**: Drive palms into the floor, fully extending elbows at the top. Squeeze chest and triceps.
5. **Breathe**: Inhale on the way down, brace hard, exhale forcefully on the press.

### Programming Recommendations

**Beginner (focused on form)**
- 10-minute EMOM of 3-5 bodyweight reps
- If you cannot complete all 10 rounds, reduce reps rather than extending rest
- Add a second EMOM block only after 4+ weeks of consistency

**Intermediate (building load)**
- 10-minute EMOM of 5 reps with 10-25 lb
- Progress load by 5-10 lb when you can complete all 10 rounds with 20+ seconds of rest remaining
- Alternate between heavier/low-rep days and lighter/high-rep days

**Advanced (maximizing density)**
- 10-minute EMOM of 3-5 reps with 45-90 lb
- Occasionally extend to 15 or 20 minutes for a volume challenge
- Pair with a pulling movement in a separate block to maintain shoulder balance

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

- Starting too heavy and failing before round 5
- Letting hips sag as fatigue sets in (indicates core weakness)
- Flaring elbows to 90 degrees (increases shoulder stress)
- Bouncing off the floor (eliminates the bottom-range strength benefit)
- Skipping the EMOM on days when push-ups feel hard (those are the most important days)

## Safety Considerations

The weighted push-up places significant demand on the wrists, shoulders, and lower back. If you experience wrist pain, try push-up handles or dumbbells to maintain a neutral wrist position. Shoulder pain often indicates elbows are flared too wide — tuck them closer to the body. Lower back sagging means your core is not adequately braced; reduce load and practice plank holds until your midline stability catches up.

## Collectible Metrics

- **Reps per round**: Target consistency across all 10 minutes
- **Load used**: Weight added for each session
- **Total reps**: Reps × 10 rounds (or adjusted if scaling)
- **Rest remaining**: Average seconds left after completing reps each minute
- **Quality rating**: Self-assess body line and depth consistency on a 1-10 scale
