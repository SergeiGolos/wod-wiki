# Geoff Neupert Kettlebell Workouts

**Category**: Strength & Conditioning / Kettlebell Specialist  
**Founder**: Geoff Neupert  
**Philosophy**: "Build a better body through intelligent kettlebell training." - Structured progression with focus on results.

---

## Overview

Geoff Neupert is a Master StrongFirst Certified Instructor (SFG II) and former RKC Team Leader who has developed numerous kettlebell programs focused on building strength, muscle, and conditioning efficiently. His programs are known for their structured approach, clear progressions, and time-efficient formats that respect the busy lives of his clients. Geoff's programming philosophy centers on "minimum effective dose" - using the least amount of training necessary to achieve the desired result.

Geoff Neupert's contributions to kettlebell training include programs like "Kettlebell Muscle," "Kettlebell Burn," "Easy Muscle," and numerous complex-based programs. His approach often uses complexes - sequences of movements performed without setting the weight down - to maximize training efficiency. He emphasizes that most people don't need more complicated programs; they need consistently executed, well-designed programs that fit into their lives.

## Workout 1: Double Kettlebell Clean and Press

**Category**: Geoff Neupert Strength  
**Type**: AMRAP Strength  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

This straightforward yet brutally effective workout uses the double kettlebell clean and press to build total-body strength and pressing power. The format is simple: perform as many quality sets of 5 reps as possible within a 20-minute window. The clean provides explosive hip power and full-body coordination, while the press builds overhead strength and shoulder stability.

The beauty of this workout lies in its simplicity and scalability. By setting a fixed time window and tracking total sets, practitioners can objectively measure progress over time. The goal is not to move as fast as possible but to accumulate quality volume with adequate rest between sets. Weight selection is critical - heavy enough to challenge, light enough to maintain form throughout.

```wod
20:00 AMRAP
  5 Double Clean & Press 24kg
  :60 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 20 minutes
- **Exercises**: Double Clean & Press
- **Goal**: Maximum quality sets in 20 minutes

### Progression Standards

| Level | Weight (Men) | Sets Target |
|-------|--------------|-------------|
| Beginner | 2x16kg | 10+ sets |
| Intermediate | 2x20kg | 12+ sets |
| Advanced | 2x24kg | 15+ sets |
| Elite | 2x28kg | 15+ sets |

### Collectible Metrics

- Total sets completed
- Weight used
- Quality of each set

## Workout 2: Kettlebell Muscle Complex 1

**Category**: Geoff Neupert Hypertrophy  
**Type**: Strength Complex  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

This complex from Geoff Neupert's "Kettlebell Muscle" program combines pressing, squatting, and pulling movements to create a full-body hypertrophy stimulus. The complex is performed as a continuous sequence without setting the bells down, creating significant metabolic demand while building muscle across multiple movement patterns.

The sequence flows naturally from one movement to the next: cleans bring the bells to the rack, presses develop overhead strength, squats build leg power, and rows provide pulling balance. The continuous nature of the complex means cardiovascular conditioning is developed alongside muscular strength and endurance.

```wod
(4)
  + 2 Double Clean 20kg
  + 2 Double Press 20kg
  + 2 Double Front Squat 20kg
  + 2 Bent Over Row 20kg
  :90 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 4 rounds
- **Exercises**: Double Clean, Double Press, Double Front Squat, Bent Over Row
- **Goal**: Complete all rounds with perfect form

### Collectible Metrics

- Rounds completed
- Weight used
- Rest needed between rounds

## Workout 3: The Universe (Light Day)

**Category**: Geoff Neupert Fat Loss  
**Type**: Complex for Fat Loss  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate

### Description

"The Universe" is a 6-week fat loss program that uses double kettlebell complexes to create significant metabolic demand. The Light Day version serves as the entry point, with Moderate and Heavy days providing progressive overload. The complex flows through multiple movements, keeping the heart rate elevated while building strength.

The program structure involves three training days per week (Light, Moderate, Heavy) with the complex evolving in complexity and intensity over the six weeks. This progressive approach allows for adaptation while continuously challenging the body, making it effective for both fat loss and strength development.

```wod
(3) Light Day
  + 2 Double Clean 16kg
  + 1 Double Press 16kg
  + 2 Double Front Squat 16kg
  + 2 Double Snatch 16kg
  :60 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 3-5 rounds
- **Exercises**: Double Clean, Double Press, Double Front Squat, Double Snatch
- **Goal**: Fat loss and conditioning

### Weekly Structure

| Day | Intensity | Rounds |
|-----|-----------|--------|
| Light | 70% | 3-4 |
| Moderate | 80% | 4-5 |
| Heavy | 90% | 5-6 |

### Collectible Metrics

- Rest periods
- Quality maintained
- Body composition changes

## Workout 4: Secret Service Snatch Test

**Category**: Geoff Neupert / StrongFirst Standard  
**Type**: Max Reps Endurance Test  
**Difficulty**: Advanced

### Description

The Secret Service Snatch Test (SSST) is a legendary kettlebell test of strength endurance. The goal is to complete as many snatches as possible in 10 minutes with a 24kg kettlebell (16kg for women). This test was allegedly used by the U.S. Secret Service as a fitness standard, hence the name.

The test challenges both the alactic and aerobic energy systems while demanding technical proficiency to maintain efficiency over 10 continuous minutes. Successful completion requires strategic pacing, efficient technique, and significant conditioning. Geoff Neupert has published various training protocols to help athletes prepare for and pass this demanding test.

```wod
10:00 ? KB Snatch 24kg
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 10 minutes continuous
- **Exercises**: Kettlebell Snatch
- **Goal**: Maximum reps in 10 minutes

### Standards

| Level | Men (24kg) | Women (16kg) |
|-------|------------|--------------|
| Beginner | 100+ reps | 80+ reps |
| Intermediate | 150+ reps | 120+ reps |
| Advanced | 200+ reps | 150+ reps |
| Elite | 250+ reps | 180+ reps |

### Collectible Metrics

- Total reps in 10 minutes
- Reps per arm
- Pacing strategy effectiveness

## Workout 5: 2 Kettlebells 12 Weeks Program

**Category**: Geoff Neupert Progressive Program  
**Type**: Progressive Strength Complex  
**Difficulty**: Beginner to Advanced

### Description

This year-long program takes practitioners from beginner through advanced double kettlebell complexes across 12 weeks. The program uses 15-minute sessions, 5 days per week, with a focus on progressive complexity and load. Each week introduces new elements while building on previous foundations.

The program is designed for those committed to long-term development, with each phase building on the previous one. Starting with basic double movements, the program progresses to complex sequences that challenge strength, coordination, and conditioning simultaneously.

```wod
Week 1-4 Foundation
(5)
  3 Double Clean 16kg
  :30 Rest

Week 5-8 Development
(5)
  + 2 Double Clean 20kg
  + 2 Double Press 20kg
  :45 Rest

Week 9-12 Advanced
(5)
  + 2 Double Clean 24kg
  + 2 Double Press 24kg
  + 3 Double Front Squat 24kg
  :60 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 12 weeks, 15 minutes per session
- **Exercises**: Progressive double kettlebell movements
- **Goal**: Mastery of double kettlebell training

### Collectible Metrics

- Weight progression
- Complex completion
- Session consistency

## Workout 6: Easy Muscle

**Category**: Geoff Neupert Hypertrophy  
**Type**: Progressive Hypertrophy  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate

### Description

Easy Muscle is Geoff Neupert's hypertrophy-focused program designed specifically for men over 40, though effective for all ages. The program builds muscle through strategic volume and tension, using sub-maximal weights and frequent practice rather than training to failure.

The program uses a "reverse ladder" structure, starting with higher reps and progressively decreasing as fatigue sets in, allowing for maximum total volume without excessive fatigue. This approach builds muscle while respecting recovery capacity, making it sustainable for long-term practice.

```wod
(3)
  10 Double Clean 20kg
  :30 Rest
  8 Double Press 20kg
  :30 Rest
  6 Double Front Squat 20kg
  :30 Rest
  4 Double Clean 20kg
  :30 Rest
  2 Double Press 20kg
  :60 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 3 rounds
- **Exercises**: Double Clean, Double Press, Double Front Squat
- **Goal**: Build muscle with sustainable training

### Collectible Metrics

- Weight used
- Total volume
- Recovery quality

## Workout 7: Swing Hard

**Category**: Geoff Neupert Conditioning  
**Type**: High Volume Swing  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate

### Description

Swing Hard is a focused swing program designed to build explosive power and conditioning through high-volume swing practice. The program uses various swing protocols including one-arm, two-arm, and hand-to-hand swings to develop the posterior chain and cardiovascular fitness.

The swing is the foundation of kettlebell training, and this program builds mastery through focused, progressive practice. By concentrating on this single movement, practitioners develop powerful hips, a strong back, and exceptional conditioning.

```wod
(10)
  10 Two-Hand KB Swing 24kg
  :20 Rest

(10)
  10 One-Arm KB Swing Left 24kg
  10 One-Arm KB Swing Right 24kg
  :30 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 15-20 minutes
- **Exercises**: Two-Hand Swing, One-Arm Swing
- **Goal**: Build swing volume and power

### Collectible Metrics

- Total swing count
- Weight used
- Power output quality
