## Warm Up

Warm Up: run ?mile OR row ?m OR bike ?mile. Then practice your parkour roll. If you have no training with a pk roll, or need some help with your roll, use this progression from Ryan Doyle:

Perform at least 50 rolls using both sides of your body. I cannot put enough emphasis on learning how to roll properly and instinctually. The pk roll has literally kept me from seriously injuring myself, and is a skill absolutely necessary to taking your training outside.

## WOD

### Étudiant

```wod
(10) :? AMRAP / 1 Parkour Roll (each side) / 5 Pushups / 10 Air Squats / 15 Sit Ups
```

### Avancée

```wod
(10) :? AMRAP / 1 Parkour Roll (each side) / 5 Pullups / 10 Pushups / 15 Air Squats
```

### Traceur

```wod
(20) :? AMRAP / 1 Parkour Roll (each side) / 5 Pullups / 10 Pushups / 15 Air Squats
```

**Note:** When performing this WOD, TAKE YOUR TIME WHEN PERFORMING THE PK ROLL. Concentrate on the form and technique, then bust through the rest of the round. Parkour rolls can be dangerous if not done correctly. Do not flip over your neck, or over the center of your back. If ANY part of the roll hurts you are doing it wrong. Stop the timer, and practice the roll until you can perform it pain free.

## Cool Down

100 parkour rolls. Either side or alternating.
