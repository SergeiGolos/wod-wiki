# Olympic IM Championship

**Type**: Elite Individual Medley  
**Duration**: 115-130 minutes  
**Difficulty**: Olympic  

### Description
This world-class IM workout prepares athletes for Olympic competition in the 200m and 400m individual medley events. The session develops exceptional proficiency across all four competitive strokes while building the specific metabolic adaptations required for multi-stroke racing at the Olympic level. Athletes must develop not only individual stroke excellence but also transition mastery and tactical intelligence that allows them to execute race strategies under immense pressure.

The training philosophy for Olympic IM emphasizes developing all four strokes to world-class levels while maintaining the athlete's natural stroke strengths. Weak strokes receive additional focus in training, but never at the expense of losing speed in stronger events. The goal is to become a true "four-stroke swimmer" rather than a specialist who survives one or two strokes.

```wod
(3) Warmup
  200m IM
  :30 Rest

(8) Butterfly Olympic
  50m Butterfly
  :30 Rest

(8) Backstroke Olympic
  50m Backstroke
  :30 Rest

(8) Breaststroke Olympic
  50m Breaststroke
  :30 Rest

(8) Freestyle Olympic
  50m Freestyle
  :30 Rest

(8) IM Race Pace
  100m IM
  :45 Rest

(3) Broken 400 Olympic
  + 100m Butterfly
  + :15 Rest
  + 100m Backstroke
  + :15 Rest
  + 100m Breaststroke
  + :15 Rest
  + 100m Freestyle
  + 4:00 Rest

(8) Transition Excellence
  75m IM Transitions
  :30 Rest

400m Cooldown
```

### Breakdown
- **Warm-up**: 600m IM continuous activation
- **Butterfly Olympic**: 400m at 200m IM butterfly pace
- **Backstroke Olympic**: 400m maintaining IM backstroke tempo
- **Breaststroke Olympic**: 400m stroke-specific power
- **Freestyle Olympic**: 400m finishing speed development
- **IM Race Pace**: 800m at goal 200m IM Olympic pace
- **Broken 400 Olympic**: 1,200m total race simulation
- **Transition Excellence**: 600m smooth stroke changes
- **Cooldown**: 400m easy swimming

### Collectible Metrics
- Stroke-specific split times at race pace
- Transition time between strokes
- Cumulative fatigue impact measurement
- Stroke count per 50m for each stroke
- Heart rate response patterns
- Technical efficiency ratings
