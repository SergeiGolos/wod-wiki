# Olympic Games Race Week

**Type**: Competition Peak  
**Duration**: 50-60 minutes  
**Difficulty**: Olympic  

### Description
This race-week session prepares Olympic athletes for maximum performance during the Games themselves. The workout is designed to prime the neuromuscular system while maintaining complete freshness for competition. Athletes complete this session during the days leading up to their events, adjusting timing based on preliminaries schedule and individual recovery needs.

The psychological component of this session is as important as the physical preparation. Athletes should visualize their Olympic race during every race-pace effort, rehearsing tactical decisions, emotional responses, and technical focus points. The session should leave athletes feeling confident, sharp, and ready to perform at their absolute best when it matters most.

```wod
(2) Warmup
  200m Freestyle
  :30 Rest

(3) Activation Build
  75m Build
  :30 Rest

(2) Race Pace Primary
  100m Race Pace
  3:00 Rest

(3) Sprint Primers
  25m Sprint
  1:30 Rest

(2) Race Pace Sharpening
  50m Goal Pace
  2:00 Rest

(3) Start Focus
  15m Dive
  1:00 Rest

(2) Pace Confirmation
  50m Goal Pace
  1:00 Rest

150m Cooldown
```

### Breakdown
- **Warm-up**: 400m easy swimming to prepare body
- **Activation Build**: 225m progressive to race pace
- **Race Pace Primary**: 200m at exact goal Olympic pace
- **Sprint Primers**: 75m neural activation at maximum
- **Race Pace Sharpening**: 100m confirming pace feel
- **Start Focus**: 45m dive technique refinement
- **Pace Confirmation**: 100m final pace verification
- **Cooldown**: 150m easy recovery

### Collectible Metrics
- Exact times on all race-pace efforts
- Stroke rate precision
- Start reaction times
- Technical execution rating (1-10)
- Confidence and readiness assessment
- Subjective energy and alertness levels
