# Mark Wildman Kettlebell Workouts

**Category**: Athletic Kettlebell Training  
**Founder**: Mark Wildman  
**Philosophy**: "You are an athlete, train like it." - Heavy club and kettlebell training for functional strength and mobility.

---

## Overview

Mark Wildman is a celebrity trainer based in Los Angeles who specializes in heavy club and kettlebell training. His approach emphasizes mobility as the foundation of health and athleticism, using progressive programming to build functional strength that transfers to daily life and sport. Mark's YouTube channel has become one of the most comprehensive resources for kettlebell technique and programming available.

Mark's programming philosophy is built around the "Tetris of Training" concept - a systematic approach to organizing workouts that balances heavy and light days, different movement patterns, and progressive overload. His programs are designed for longevity, teaching practitioners to train sustainably rather than constantly pushing to failure. The emphasis on heavy club training alongside kettlebells provides unique benefits for shoulder health and rotational power.

## Workout 1: The Man Maker (Full Body)

**Category**: Mark Wildman Signature  
**Type**: Full Body Strength  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate

### Description

The Man Maker is Mark Wildman's signature full-body workout that combines multiple movement patterns into a comprehensive training session. The workout flows through hinges, squats, pushes, and pulls, creating balanced development in a time-efficient format.

The session is designed to be progressively overloaded through weight increases and rep additions. Mark emphasizes quality over quantity, teaching that sustainable progress comes from perfect practice rather than maximum effort in every session.

```wod
The Man Maker:
(4)
  10 Two-Hand KB Swing 24kg
  :30 Rest

(3)
  8 Clean & Press Left 20kg
  8 Clean & Press Right 20kg
  :45 Rest

(3)
  10 Goblet Squat 24kg
  :30 Rest

(3)
  10 Bent Over Row Left 20kg
  10 Bent Over Row Right 20kg
  :30 Rest

Finisher:
(2)
  15 Two-Hand KB Swing 24kg
  :20 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 25-30 minutes
- **Exercises**: Swing, Clean & Press, Goblet Squat, Row
- **Goal**: Full-body strength and conditioning

### Collectible Metrics

- Weight progression
- Total volume
- Session consistency

## Workout 2: Heavy/Light Program Design

**Category**: Mark Wildman Programming System  
**Type**: Progressive Strength  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

The Heavy/Light programming system is Mark Wildman's approach to organizing training for sustainable progress. The concept alternates between heavier, lower-volume sessions and lighter, higher-volume sessions, allowing for quality practice and recovery while still making progress.

Heavy days focus on building absolute strength with heavier weights and lower reps. Light days focus on technique refinement and work capacity with lighter weights and higher reps. This alternation allows for frequent training without burnout while developing both strength and endurance.

```wod
Heavy Day:
(5)
  3 Clean & Press 28kg
  :60 Rest

(5)
  3 Goblet Squat 32kg
  :60 Rest

(5)
  5 Pullups
  :60 Rest

Light Day:
(5)
  8 Clean & Press 20kg
  :45 Rest

(5)
  10 Goblet Squat 24kg
  :45 Rest

(5)
  8 Pushups
  :30 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: Alternate Heavy/Light, 3-4 sessions per week
- **Exercises**: Clean & Press, Goblet Squat, Pull-ups/Push-ups
- **Goal**: Build strength while maintaining technique

### Weekly Structure

| Day | Focus | Weight | Reps |
|-----|-------|--------|------|
| Day 1 | Heavy | 85-90% | 3-5 |
| Day 2 | Light | 60-70% | 8-12 |
| Day 3 | Heavy | 85-90% | 3-5 |
| Day 4 | Light | 60-70% | 8-12 |

### Collectible Metrics

- Heavy day weight progression
- Light day volume
- Recovery quality between sessions

## Workout 3: The Two Best Exercises

**Category**: Mark Wildman Minimalist  
**Type**: Minimalist Training  
**Difficulty**: All Levels

### Description

Mark Wildman has identified the two "best" kettlebell exercises that everyone should do forever: the swing and the Turkish get-up. This minimalist program uses only these two movements to build comprehensive fitness. The swing develops powerful hips and posterior chain strength, while the get-up builds total-body coordination, shoulder stability, and core strength.

This program represents Mark's philosophy that simple, consistent practice of fundamental movements produces better results than complex programming. The minimalist approach allows for mastery of these essential patterns while fitting into any schedule.

```wod
Daily Practice:
(5)
  10 Two-Hand KB Swing 24kg
  :30 Rest

(3)
  1 Turkish Get Up Left 16kg
  1 Turkish Get Up Right 16kg
  :60 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 10-15 minutes
- **Exercises**: Two-Hand Swing, Turkish Get-Up
- **Goal**: Daily practice for long-term development

### Progression

| Phase | Swing Weight | TGU Weight | Frequency |
|-------|--------------|------------|-----------|
| 1 | 16-20kg | 8-12kg | 3x/week |
| 2 | 20-24kg | 12-16kg | 4x/week |
| 3 | 24-32kg | 16-24kg | 5x/week |

### Collectible Metrics

- Weight progression
- Quality of movement
- Weekly consistency

## Workout 4: BJJ Strength Program

**Category**: Mark Wildman Sport-Specific  
**Type**: Sport-Specific Strength  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

This program is designed specifically for Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu practitioners, building strength and conditioning that transfers to the mat while respecting the recovery needs of athletes who also train BJJ regularly. The program focuses on movements that develop grip strength, hip power, and core stability - all essential for grappling.

The programming avoids excessive soreness and fatigue that would interfere with BJJ training while still building the strength attributes that improve performance. Movements are selected for their transfer to grappling situations.

```wod
BJJ Strength Session:
(4)
  10 KB Swing 24kg
  :30 Rest

(3)
  8 Gorilla Row Left 20kg
  8 Gorilla Row Right 20kg
  :30 Rest

(3)
  10 Goblet Squat 24kg
  :30 Rest

(3)
  :30 Farmers Carry 24kg
  :30 Rest

(3)
  5 Turkish Get Up Left 16kg
  5 Turkish Get Up Right 16kg
  :60 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 25-30 minutes
- **Exercises**: Swing, Gorilla Row, Goblet Squat, Farmers Carry, TGU
- **Goal**: Build grappling-specific strength

### Collectible Metrics

- Grip endurance
- Hip power
- Core stability improvements

## Workout 5: Fat Loss Protocol (Beginner to Advanced)

**Category**: Mark Wildman Fat Loss  
**Type**: Progressive Conditioning  
**Difficulty**: All Levels

### Description

This fat loss protocol progresses from beginner to advanced through three levels of intensity. Each level uses the same fundamental movements but with increasing volume, weight, or density. The focus is on sustainable fat loss through consistent effort rather than extreme intervention.

The protocol combines swings, squats, and pressing movements to create metabolic demand while building strength. The progressive format allows practitioners to advance at their own pace while always having a clear next step.

```wod
Beginner:
(3)
  10 KB Swing 16kg
  10 Goblet Squat 16kg
  :45 Rest

Intermediate:
(5)
  15 KB Swing 20kg
  15 Goblet Squat 20kg
  :30 Rest

Advanced:
(5)
  20 KB Swing 24kg
  15 Clean & Press 20kg
  15 Goblet Squat 24kg
  :30 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 15-25 minutes based on level
- **Exercises**: Swing, Goblet Squat, Clean & Press
- **Goal**: Sustainable fat loss with strength maintenance

### Collectible Metrics

- Weight progression
- Rest period reduction
- Body composition changes

## Workout 6: Tetris of Training (Program Design)

**Category**: Mark Wildman Programming  
**Type**: Balanced Programming System  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate

### Description

The Tetris of Training is Mark Wildman's systematic approach to program design that organizes training around movement patterns rather than body parts. The system ensures balanced development by categorizing exercises and distributing them appropriately across training sessions.

The concept uses a horizontal and vertical organization: horizontally organizing by movement pattern (hinge, squat, push, pull), and vertically by intensity (heavy, medium, light). This creates a framework that can accommodate any exercise while ensuring nothing is neglected.

```wod
Day 1 - Hinge Focus:
(5)
  10 KB Swing 24kg
  :30 Rest
(3)
  10 Clean & Press 20kg
  :45 Rest

Day 2 - Squat Focus:
(5)
  10 Goblet Squat 24kg
  :30 Rest
(3)
  10 Two-Hand KB Swing 24kg
  :30 Rest

Day 3 - Push/Pull:
(5)
  8 Clean & Press 20kg
  :45 Rest
(5)
  8 Gorilla Row 20kg
  :45 Rest
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 3-4 sessions per week
- **Exercises**: Rotating based on pattern focus
- **Goal**: Balanced, progressive training

### Movement Categories

| Pattern | Examples |
|---------|----------|
| Hinge | Swing, Deadlift, Clean |
| Squat | Goblet Squat, Front Squat |
| Push | Press, Push-up |
| Pull | Row, Pull-up |

### Collectible Metrics

- Balance across patterns
- Weekly volume distribution
- Recovery quality
