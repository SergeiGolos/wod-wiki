## Workout 4: The 40-Day Program

**Category**: Dan John Strength Program  
**Type**: Progressive Strength  
**Difficulty**: Intermediate to Advanced

### Description

The 40-Day Program is Dan John's approach to "Easy Strength" - building strength without excessive fatigue or soreness. The program uses sub-maximal weights performed frequently, typically 5 sets of 2 reps or 2 sets of 5 reps, done 5 days per week for 40 days. The focus is on perfect repetitions and building strength through frequency rather than intensity.

The beauty of the 40-Day Program lies in its sustainability. Because the weights are sub-maximal and the volume is moderate, recovery is rapid, allowing for daily practice. Over time, the frequent exposure to the movements builds neural efficiency, technical mastery, and surprisingly strong strength gains without the burnout associated with high-intensity programs.

```wod
(5) Easy Strength Day
  + 2 Double Clean 24kg
  + 2 Double Press 24kg
  + 2 Double Front Squat 24kg
  :60 Rest

(5) Pull-ups

(5) Hanging Leg Raises
```

### Breakdown

- **Rounds/Duration**: 40 consecutive days
- **Exercises**: Clean, Press, Front Squat, Pull-ups, Core
- **Goal**: Build strength without soreness

### Principles

1. Never miss a rep
2. Never strain excessively
3. Keep the same weight until 40 days complete
4. Practice, don't "work out"

### Collectible Metrics

- Daily consistency
- Quality of each rep
- Subjective energy levels
